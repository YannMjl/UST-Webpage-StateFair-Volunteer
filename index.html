Group Visits

Each year the University of St. Thomas welcomes a diverse range of high schools, charter schools and community-based organizations to our St. Paul campus. We do our best to accommodate as many groups as possible.

Please review our group visit policy before requesting your visit to St. Thomas.

Group Visits policy

Group visits will not be accommodated:

  • Mondays or Fridays
  • During admission events
  • The week of midterms
  • During university breaks
  • The months of April and May
  • On weekends
  • At the Minneapolis campus

    • Group visit requests will be reviewed on a first-come, first-served basis.

    Requests for student groups younger than ninth grade should be directed to the St. Thomas Volunteer Center. They can be reached at community@stthomas.edu or (651) 962-5262.

    A group visit consists of:

  • A 30-minute admissions presentation
  • A one-hour campus tour

    • The University of St. Thomas does not pay for group transportation or meals. For those groups willing to pay for their own meals while on campus, the Office of Admissions can assist the group with on-campus meal arrangements.

    Please bring one adult chaperone for every 10 students. It is the responsibility of each chaperone to monitor the behavior of their students. Inadequate student conduct or adult supervision, can result in a group losing future visit privileges.
